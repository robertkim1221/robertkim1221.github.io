Vision-Based Defect Detection for Solar Panel Maintenance Robots (2021)

The Vision-Based Defect Detection for Solar Panel Maintenance Robots was part of a multi-company consortium project funded by South Koreaâ€™s Ministry of Trade, Industry, and Energy. The project aimed to develop an autonomous robot capable of fault diagnosis and cleaning of solar panels in large-scale power plants. As part of this initiative, I was responsible for developing the perception module to inspect solar panels for defects and pollution. The system was deployed at Korea East-West Power Plant, where the robot autonomously moved along rows of solar panels, scanning for anomalies in real-time.

Project Overview

The system relied on a combination of thermal and visual sensors to detect faults. I integrated IR thermal cameras to identify overheating or defective solar panels and CCD image sensors with wide-angle lenses to capture high-resolution visual data. To process this data effectively, I developed a solar panel imaging pipeline that used homographies for geometric correction, ensuring accurate alignment of image data.

For defect detection, I implemented computer vision and image processing techniques using OpenCV. The system was designed to detect cracks, hotspots, and dirt accumulation using custom thresholding algorithms and feature extraction. Additionally, I incorporated YOLO-based object detection to classify different types of panel defects with high precision.

In terms of hardware, I designed a modular sensor module enclosed in an IP34-rated housing, making it resistant to dust and water for outdoor deployment. The casing was optimized to protect the thermal and visual sensors from harsh environmental conditions, ensuring reliable performance in real-world power plant settings.
